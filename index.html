Innovator Founder Visa — frequently asked questions

Are you an immigration lawyer?
No, and I'm clear about that. I don't provide immigration legal advice. What I provide is business plan review and strategy from the perspective of someone who successfully went through the endorsement process. For legal advice, you should work with a qualified immigration solicitor — and I can point you in the right direction.
What types of businesses do you review?
I specialise in SaaS, AI, and technology businesses — that's my domain expertise. If your business is in a different sector, get in touch — the endorsement criteria framework applies broadly, and I'll let you know upfront if I'm the right fit.
Can you guarantee endorsement?
No one can guarantee endorsement — be wary of anyone who claims they can. What I can do is significantly improve your chances by identifying the gaps that typically lead to rejection and helping you address them before you submit.
I haven't started my business plan yet. Can you help?
This service is a review, not a writing service. You should have at least a solid draft before booking. If you're just starting out, I'd recommend joining r/InnovatorFounder on Reddit where I share free resources and guidance.
How long does the whole process take?
After the free intro call, you send your plan and I deliver the written review within 7 business days. The walkthrough call is typically scheduled within the following week. From there, you revise with my support, and once your plan is ready I coach you for the interview. Most clients go from first call to submission-ready within 3–5 weeks, depending on how much revision is needed.
What if I've already been rejected?
That's actually a very common scenario — and one I understand personally. Bring your rejection feedback along with your plan. I'll help you understand what went wrong and how to address it for your next attempt.
